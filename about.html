Article 01- Karl Heinz-Legler, achieves industry recognition

Created on Tuesday, 02 August 2016

The GFG's member for Canada, Rutherford Global Logistics, harbours an industry star in Karl Heinz-Legler...

I should have realised that there was more to this story than first it seemed when I did a simple search for ‘Karl’ on the Canadian Sailings website and ended up with two pages of results, each denoting an article featuring said ‘Karl’.

Karl has been busy!

I shan’t try to cover all the articles here, suffice it to say you can find them all here: http://www.canadiansailings.ca

So where to start?
It would be an understatement to say that Karl has been heavily involved in Canadian freight.

When he is not handling freight in his first official capacity as General Manager of the Rutherford Global Logistics’ Eastern Canada, he is volunteering his expertise, experience and services to affiliated industry organisations such as CIFFA and I.E. Canada, the Canadian Importers and Exporters Association.

Let us first understand Karl’s official role and the resulting link with the Global Freight Group.

Rutherford Global Logistics has been the exclusive Canadian member of the GFG since 1997.

As well as Rutherford’s loyalty to the GFG as a member, their additional commitment of time and resources also deserves mention. Rutherford are one of the very few members who have hosted not one, but two, of the GFG’s annual conferences.

With 22 offices across Canada, the family owned business has a fine selection of locations to entice the GFG members to the annual GFG conference. Toronto was the location for Rutherford’s first hosting experience, in 2002, and Vancouver for its second in 2013. The welcoming Canadian team and fantastic locations resulted in the Rutherford team hosting two very memorable events for the GFG and its members. (Learn more about Rutherford Global Logistics here.

Karl has represented Rutherford Global Logistics at several Global Freight Group annual conferences, making friends on an international level with his endearing, easy manner and smile. Surrounded by freight forwarders from across the globe, he is in his element. Networking comes naturally to him.

One of Karl’s unofficial industry roles is that of member of the Canadian Freight Forwarders National Sea Freight Committee. Within this role Karl was a driving force representing CIFFA and Canadian freight forwarders on the Canadian Stakeholder Group in discussions with Authorities, Ports, Carriers and Terminals alike and advice regarding the Canadian implementation guidelines for the new IMO SOLAS VGM regulations.

Current CIFFA President, Mr J Cullen, felt that Karl’s input and participation warranted special mention. Accordingly, Karl’s volunteer work was recognised at CIFFA’s 67th Annual General Meeting on 16th June 2016, where Mr Cullen presented Karl with a plaque in thanks for his valued contributions.

CIFFA wasn’t the only industry association to recognise Karl’s important role in researching, discussing and representing Canada and freight forwarders regarding all the latest developments in industry regulations. I.E. CANADA through their CATIE (Canadian Awards for Trade in Import and Export) also appreciated the value of Karl’s work and saw fit to recognise him accordingly, citing him for their Trade Compliance Leadership Award.

The 2016 CATIE award for Trade Compliance Leadership could have been tailor-made to suit Karl’s role in the freight industry. The recent developments and implementation of IMO SOLAS VGM have been the perfect example of what Karl does to support the logistics industry – gathering information and disseminating it, ensuring that all those who need to know do know.

To recognize a Canadian customs compliance and trade professional who has been providing leadership, innovation and advocacy on behalf of their own company and other importers and exporters in Canada.

The new regulations have entailed serious debate and input from various entities and Karl has made sure that he not only put forward pertinent points on behalf of all those in the industry but he also ensured that he relayed all the progress back to Rutherford, the GFG and his other contacts in the industry. He has truly been a linchpin for developments.

Karl has been instrumental in keeping the GFG members updated with regard to SOLAS, as you can see from the GFG’s own advisory page.



Article 02- USA Cargo & GFS 3 months on...

Created on Tuesday, 19 April 2016

Back in February we reported that the GFG's Atlanta export member, USA Cargo Services Co. had acquired Global Freight Services. Read the original article here. We are delighted to bring you an update, full of positive progress...

Let it not be said that the GFG Atlanta member lets the grass grow under its feet!

Indeed, only 3 months after acquiring Global Freight Services, USA Cargo are welcoming lots of new business, as a result of the new acquisition.

USA Cargo’s Airfreight Manager, Mike McCauley, reported that, within a week, they had secured their first airfreight shipment from one of their new customers, moving from France to ATL. Mike also confirmed that a second new customer has provided a shipment to Argentina.

With new freight business confirmed with France and Argentina, the GFG’s Atlanta member is certainly flexing its tentacles both far east across the Atlantic and also far south of their Atlanta HQ. The GFG members there, Thales in France and ACSA Srl in Argentina, are both reaping the rewards that USA Cargo’s new business offers.

Whilst USA Cargo have long been experts at shipping globally, their acquisition of GFS is certainly bringing in some new business in those areas.

Not only that, but in true GFG spirit, they are sharing their good fortune and hard work with other GFG members, recommending GFG members in other countries to the new customers.

USA Cargo has also passed on opportunities to the GFG member in Canada (Rutherford Global Logistics), with trade show freight being shipped back from Vancouver.

Rumour has it there is also freight on the books moving from France to MEL, Australia coming up, so the GFG’s members in both those countries can look forward to future involvement. This is the true benefit of belonging to the GFG: one member's success can benefit many others. USA Cargo’s acquisition of GFS is certainly producing global good fortune for them and the GFG members, making their international presence even more widely known. With freight movements literally spanning oceans and continents, there has been a knock on effect of good fortune around the GFG globe. If “what goes around, comes around”, the wave of good karma should be lapping at the door of USA Cargo any day now.
